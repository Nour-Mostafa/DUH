Galaxies

Galaxies are vast collections of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter, bound together by gravity. Galaxies come in a variety of shapes, including spirals, ellipticals, and irregulars, and contain billions or even trillions of stars.
The most prominent galaxy is the Milky Way, which contains our solar system. Galaxies are the building blocks of the universe, and are studied to understand the evolution and structure of the universe.

Formation of galaxies

Formation of galaxies

The formation of galaxies is a complex process that began right after the Big Bang.
Imagine the universe as a huge cloud of gas and dust. Over time, certain regions of this cloud began to become denser than others due to gravity. What happened next:
* The denser regions attracted more and more matter, creating huge gas clouds.
* Within these clouds, denser regions began to appear, causing these regions to collapse and form the first stars.
* As star formation continued, these huge gas clouds began to turn into galaxies, each containing billions of stars. In a nutshell:
Galaxies formed from huge gas clouds that were brought together by gravity, and then stars began to form within these clouds, thus giving birth to the galaxies we see today.

Types of galaxies

Types of galaxies

1- Spiral: It resembles a disk with spiral arms, an example of which is the Milky Way Galaxy, and it contains large amounts of gas and dust that form new stars
2- Elliptical: It is oval or spherical in shape and contains old stars and a small amount of gas and dust
3- Irregular: It does not have a specific shape and is often small or large galaxies and is exposed to collisions with other galaxies

Number of galaxies

Determining the number of galaxies is a difficult challenge for scientists, but previous estimates indicate the presence of about 100 to 200 billion galaxies in the visible universe. The latest estimates indicate the presence of about 100 billion galaxies, and the most optimistic estimates indicate the presence of a trillion galaxies. The factors affecting the estimation of galaxies are the difficulty of observation due to the distance of galaxies and their dimness, which makes their observation difficult. With the development of telescopes, estimates will change constantly.

Names of galaxies

Names of galaxies

1- Andromeda: It is the closest large galaxy to the Milky Way and can be seen with the naked eye on clear, dark nights
2- Cat's Eye Galaxy: It is characterized by the dust rings surrounding it that resemble a cat's eye
3- Cartwheel Galaxy: It is characterized by its wide spiral arms that resemble a cart wheel

The most important features of galaxies

The most important features of galaxies

1- Diversity of shapes and sizes: There are spiral, elliptical and irregular galaxies and their sizes range from dwarf to giant
2- Basic components: They consist of billions of stars, gas, dust and dark matter
3- Continuous rotation: Galaxies rotate around their center and the further they are from their center, the slower the rotation speed
4- Continuous development: New stars are formed, old stars die and galaxies interact with each other
5- The presence of black holes: It is believed that most large galaxies contain a black hole in their center

Interaction between galaxies

Interaction between galaxies is simply what happens when two or more galaxies come close to each other. Imagine that galaxies are huge clouds of stars, gas and dust, and when these clouds come close to each other, they begin to affect each other due to the force of gravity. What happens when galaxies interact?
* Merger: Galaxies may collide with each other and merge to form one larger galaxy. This causes great disturbance within the new galaxies, and leads to the formation of new stars.
* Deformation: The shapes of galaxies may change due to mutual gravitational forces, so they appear distorted or stretched.
* Exchange of matter: Galaxies may exchange gas and stars with each other.
Why does interaction between galaxies occur?
* Gravity: It is the main force that attracts galaxies to each other.
* Random motion: Galaxies move in space, which increases the likelihood of their collision.
Example: Our galaxy, the Milky Way, is on a date with a massive collision with the Andromeda galaxy in billions of years.

Distances between galaxies

Distances between galaxies

The universe is a very vast place, and galaxies are spread out over great distances.
Gravity works to gather matter into galaxies, but the distances between these clusters remain large.
How do we measure these distances?
We use special tools to measure distances to galaxies, such as:
Light year: is the distance that light travels in one year, and is about 9.46 trillion kilometers. *Parsec: is a unit of measurement larger than the light year, and is used to measure distances between distant celestial bodies. One parsec is equal to about 3.26 light years

What is the galaxy we live in?

What is the galaxy we live in?

It is the Milky Way Galaxy, which is a huge flat disk containing two spiral arms, and we live on one of these arms.
Why is it called the Milky Way? If you look at the sky on dark nights, far from the city lights, you will see a blurry strip of light extending across the sky. This strip is part of our galaxy, and the ancients described it as the Milky Way or the Milky Way.

تشكل المجرات

تشكل المجرات عملية معقدة بدأت مباشرة بعد الانفجار العظيم. تخيل الكون كسحابة ضخمة من الغاز والغبار. مع مرور الوقت، بدأت مناطق معينة من هذه السحابة تصبح أكثر كثافة من غيرها بسبب الجاذبية. ماذا حدث بعد ذلك: * جذبت المناطق الأكثر كثافة المزيد والمزيد من المادة، مما أدى إلى إنشاء سحب غازية ضخمة. * داخل هذه السحب، بدأت مناطق أكثر كثافة في الظهور، مما تسبب في انهيار هذه المناطق وتكوين النجوم الأولى. * مع استمرار تشكل النجوم، بدأت هذه السحب الغازية الضخمة تتحول إلى مجرات، تحتوي كل منها على مليارات النجوم. باختصار: تشكلت المجرات من سحب غازية ضخمة جمعتها الجاذبية، ثم بدأت النجوم تتشكل داخل هذه السحب، مما أدى إلى ولادة المجرات التي نراها اليوم

التفاعل بين المجرات

التفاعل بين المجرات هو ببساطة ما يحدث عندما تقترب مجرتان أو أكثر من بعضهما البعض. تخيل أن المجرات عبارة عن سحب ضخمة من النجوم والغاز والغبار، وعندما تقترب هذه السحب من بعضها البعض، تبدأ في التأثير على بعضها البعض بسبب قوة الجاذبية. ماذا يحدث عندما تتفاعل المجرات؟ * الاندماج: قد تصطدم المجرات ببعضها البعض وتندمج لتشكل مجرة ​​واحدة أكبر. وهذا يسبب اضطرابًا كبيرًا داخل المجرات الجديدة، ويؤدي إلى تكوين نجوم جديدة. * التشوه: قد تتغير أشكال المجرات بسبب قوى الجاذبية المتبادلة، فتبدو مشوهة أو ممتدة. * تبادل المادة: قد تتبادل المجرات الغاز والنجوم مع بعضها البعض. لماذا يحدث التفاعل بين المجرات؟ * الجاذبية: هي القوة الرئيسية التي تجذب المجرات إلى بعضها البعض. * الحركة العشوائية: تتحرك المجرات في الفضاء، مما يزيد من احتمالية اصطدامها. مثال: مجرتنا، درب التبانة، على موعد مع اصطدام هائل مع مجرة ​​أندروميدا في مليارات السنين.
